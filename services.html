Income Protection Insurance using Smart Contracts
How it works?

Simple and secure.

The Blockchain technology proved to be a great alternative that provides transparency, security and agility. A great innovation that we can address is the possibility of building a trustless system, based on a contract, between two people, in the form of a computer code.

Because of this, Income Protection Insurance (IPI) can benefit from this kind of technology. As a traditional IPI it would cover around 50% to 65% of a person’s income if it is unable to work as a result of an accident or illness. The insurance attribution system could be improved by directly extracting the government data that would accrue if the person is in working conditions or if it is disabled by some type of health problem and mediating this through a smart contract.

The smart contract could be signed on a monthly basis or be run by multiple months in order to provide good assistance for the person who is contracting the insurance company’s service. And the best side of that: there is no need to wait for the indemnity (for approval) or to trust any third party in the process.

Practical example of applying smart contracts at IPI:

Bob sends $100 worth of cryptocurrency to the IPI Smart Contract and provides his work identity. Then, the insurance company sends $900 to the smart contract. So there is $1000 in the IPI Smart Contract.

Next month, the IPI Smart Contract’s system checks if Bob is in working conditions or if he is sick or had an accident. If everything is fine with Bob, the insurance company gets back $1000 worth of cryptocurrencies, otherwise, Bob receives this full indemnity amount.
